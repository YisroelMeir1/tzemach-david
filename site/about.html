About

Tzemach David

A Hebrew world chronicle in two parts by Rabbi David Gans (student of the Rema and the Maharal of Prague). Part I traces Jewish history year by year from Creation to the author's day. Part II surveys general world history. First printed in Prague in 1592 — the first history book printed in Hebrew.

The author

Rabbi David Gans (רבי דוד גנז, 1541–1613) lived in Prague. He studied under the Rema and the Maharal, and wrote on history, astronomy, and mathematics. Tzemach David (Prague 1592) is his best-known work.

Structure

  • Part I — Jewish history, year by year from Creation to Gans’s generation.
  • Part II — World history (the four kingdoms of Daniel), with a famous preface on why Jews may study secular chronicles.
